About Mount Everest

Mount Everest is the world's highest mountain above sea level, located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and Tibet. It has long been a goal for mountaineers and adventurers from around the world.

History

The first successful ascent of Mount Everest was on May 29, 1953, by Sir Edmund Hillary of New Zealand and Tenzing Norgay, a Sherpa of Nepal. Since then, thousands of climbers have attempted to reach the summit, making it both a symbol of human achievement and a testament to the dangers of extreme mountaineering.

Climbing Routes

There are two main routes to the summit: the South Col route from Nepal and the North Col route from Tibet. Each route has its own challenges, including extreme altitude, severe weather, and dangerous terrain.
